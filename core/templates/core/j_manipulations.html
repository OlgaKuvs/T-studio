Joint Manipulations involve a set of actions performed on the joints of the limbs and spine with the aim of restoring impaired range of motion, alleviating pain, and stimulating the regeneration of damaged tissues. Manipulations are primarily carried out by the hands of a therapist, although special devices, tools, and other parts of the therapist's body may also be utilized.

There is a wide range of joint manipulations stemming from various schools and traditions of manual therapy, an ancient practice as old as medicine itself. Joint manipulations fall under manual therapy and are employed by chiropractors, osteopaths, manual therapists, and a variety of traditional bone-setting practitioners worldwide.

I utilize manipulations presented in manual therapy in their basic form, often seamlessly incorporating them into the massage process so that the patient may not even notice anything out of the ordinary. Other techniques require preparation, specific postures, and synchronization with breathing. In such cases, the patient is aware of joint manipulation and often hears the characteristic joint cracking sound, which is a desirable outcome of the therapy. Quick thrusts and joint cracking may initially cause apprehension for a patient undergoing the treatment for the first time. However, as trust develops in your therapist, joint manipulations begin to be perceived calmly.

Performing manipulations takes minutes, and muscles warmed and relaxed by massage do not hinder their execution as much as unprepared muscles would.

Such treatment is conducted in short courses, typically comprising 3-4 sessions, and ideally should not be repeated more frequently than once every six months. Indications, limitations, and contraindications should be considered.
