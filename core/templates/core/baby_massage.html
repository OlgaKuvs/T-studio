Baby or Infant Massage is characterized by a blend of structured touch on the skin, moderate pressure on soft tissues, and gentle movements of the limbs. Learning the basics of providing this massage to your child is easily achievable, or you may opt for professional services if you seek targeted developmental benefits for your child.

Self-administered massages offer advantages for both the child and the parent, fostering a unique bonding experience.

You are establishing a connection that can last for many years, overcoming the ingrained taboo on touch in the modern person's consciousness. As children age, they begin to feel self-conscious and avoid physical contact with their parents. Massage, which has become a tradition in your family, can overcome this artificial barrier, providing physical, emotional, and mental well-being for both parties. There are studies on animals and observations of humans that demonstrate the importance of touch for the comprehensive development of a child and for maintaining the health of parents. Psychologists also agree on the importance of regular physical contact throughout a person's life, especially during the early years.

The massage of newborns has indications and contraindications, of which there are few. Baby massage sessions are typically associated only with positive emotions in children and last approximately 30 minutes. A standard massage course usually comprises 10 sessions, with the presence of a parent during the massage being mandatory for children.
